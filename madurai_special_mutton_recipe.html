Spicy Mutton Chukka Recipe

-by Samaran.S (iam-samzz)

Hello everyone! Welcome to my page. Today, I’m going to guide you through making one of the most delicious dishes in Madurai: Mutton Chukka!

Ingredients

  • Mutton: 500g (curry cut, preferably bone-in or small boneless pieces for quick cooking)
  • For the Gravy Base: 1.5 cups small onions (shallots) (peeled and sliced), 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 2 sprigs of fresh curry leaves, 3 tbsp gingelly oil (sesame oil).

    • Fresh Chukka Masala (Roast & Grind):

  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns (adjust for heat)
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds (soambu)
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds (jeeragam)
  • 3-4 dry red chilies

Instructions

  1. Dry roast 1 tbsp coriander seeds, 1 tsp pepper, 1 tsp fennel seeds, 1/2 tsp cumin, and 4 dry red chilies until fragrant, then grind into a coarse powder.
  2. Pressure cook 500g mutton with a splash of water, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, and a pinch of salt for 4 to 5 whistles until tender. Save the broth!
  3. Heat 3 tbsp sesame oil in a pan, toss in a few curry leaves, and fry 1.5 cups of sliced small onions (shallots) until they turn soft and translucent.
  4. Stir 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste into the fried onions and sauté for a minute until the raw smell completely goes away.
  5. Add the cooked mutton, its leftover broth, and your ground spice powder to the pan. Cook on medium heat until all the liquid evaporates and the spices thicken.
  6. Fry the mixture on medium-high heat for another 3 minutes with a handful of fresh curry leaves until the meat looks dark brown and the spices tightly coat every piece.
